Research Phase

As part of the user research we also sent out a survey to gain insight into people’s thoughts and opinions about donating to an overseas charity.

Definition Phase

After user testing for the website and completing survey for how Australians felt about donating overseas, the user insight statement was formed. The user persona was then formed.

The storyboard for the user’s journey was then created.

Ideation Phase

The inspiration for the design was sparked by the black and white photography from the foundation's Payata’s Paper. This documentary style photography holds a certain truth and captures the raw honesty of living below the poverty line in Manila. We utilised this photographic style as one of the core elements for the visual design and identity to establish transparency, trust and emotional connection with the user. With the development of the brand identity we again drew inspiration from an outside source - a quote from Maya Angelou ‘Each of us has the power and responsibility to become a rainbow in the clouds.’ The logo radiates light and hope and the pops of transparent colour that appear over the black and white photography is representative of being a rainbow in the clouds for the children of Payatas.

Prototyping Phase

Creating a donation page that enables recurring donations to increase number of donations and donations amounts over time - extending lifetime value of a donator.

The ‘donate’ and ‘volunteer’ buttons are bright and bold and at the forefront of the design to make accessing the two major call-to-action items on the website easier to access by the user. Decreasing the user’s time searching for these opportunities increases revenue and volunteer opportunities.
